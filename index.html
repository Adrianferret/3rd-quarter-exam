RECYCLING AND WASTE MANAGEMENT
by Adrian R. Blaza


Did you know that over 7,000,000 MILLION PEOPLE DIE from AIR POLLUTION
annually?

Tragic right? but you can help!

You can help by RECYCLING and managing your WASTE

here are some ways to help!
Only buy what you need and use what you buy.
Buy loose fruit and vegetables and avoid heavily packaged items.
Sign up for paperless billing or banking.
Choose products with refillable packaging.
Try home composting your food waste.
Use refillable water bottles and coffee cups.
Reuse or repurpose items.
Limit use of single-use and disposable products.
Opt for products with minimal packaging.
Donate or sell old clothing, electronics, and building materials.


1. Paper Cardboard boxes: Flatten and recycle them in the proper bin.
Newspapers: Make sure they are clean and dry before recycling.
Magazines & catalogs: Most curbside programs accept them.
Junk mail: Sort out any plastics or metal and recycle the paper.

2. Plastic Bottles: Recycle water, soda, and cleaning product bottles.
Containers: Recycle food containers like yogurt or takeout boxes after rinsing.
Plastic bags: Many grocery stores accept plastic bags for recycling.
Food packaging: Ensure it's clean and dry before recycling.

3. Glass Bottles: Recycle glass beverage and food jars.
Jars: Clean and dry before placing them in the bin.
Containers: Check local guidelines for specific glass types that can be recycled.

4. Metal Aluminum cans: Soda, beer, and other drink cans are recyclable.
Tin cans: Rinsed food cans are accepted in most recycling programs.
Scrap metal: Certain local centers accept metal scrap, like old appliances or tools.

5. Electronics (E-Waste)
Old phones, computers, and gadgets: Many stores and recycling centers offer programs for recycling old electronics.
Batteries: Rechargeable batteries should be properly disposed of or taken to a specialized recycling program.
Cables: Old cables and chargers can be recycled at some electronics stores.

6. Organic Waste
Composting: Fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and yard waste can be composted for gardening.
Food scraps: Many communities have composting programs for food waste.

7. Clothing and Textiles
Donate: Gently used clothes can be donated to charity.
Upcycling: Repurpose old clothes into new items or crafts.
Textile recycling bins: Some facilities recycle worn-out clothes, shoes, and bedding.

8. Batteries
Rechargeable batteries: Special collection points exist for batteries from electronics.
Car batteries: Take them to a recycling facility to ensure they are safely disposed of.

9. Compact Fluorescent Bulbs (CFLs) and Light Bulbs
CFL recycling: Most hardware stores offer recycling for energy-saving light bulbs.
LED bulbs: Some recycling programs accept old LED bulbs.

10. Furniture
Donations: Old furniture in good condition can be donated to thrift stores or charity organizations. Recycling centers: Some centers will accept furniture for repurposing.
